Project

Instacart Basket Analysis


1. Overview


Instacart - an online grocery delivery service - has a wealth of knowledge about their user and orders and wants to analyze this information to inform their marketing decisions and improve their targeted advertising strategy. They would like to drill down on customer purchasing behavior based on region, family status, order time and day, repeating orders, and other factors that could help fine-tune marketing strategy and increase sales.

Data: Instacart product and orders datasets Customer and price datasets


2. Objectives


Discovering more details around sales patterns Perform exploratory analysis of data to derive learnings Suggest strategies for improved customer and order segmentations Answer key business questions, including: Busiest days of the week and hours of the day for optimal ad scheduling When people spend the most money Which types of products are most popular Which departments have the highest frequency of product orders Differing customer profiles based on ordering behavior and demographics Analyze data to create data-driven marketing strategies Discover new insights about ordering habits and create customer profiles


3. Process


Exploratory data analysis Data cleaning, wrangling, and subsetting, including renaming columns for clarity, dropping unnecessary columns, or those containing personal identifying information Performing consistency checks for missing or duplicate values Merging datasets once cleaned and wrangled Deriving new variables and creating flags, such as for customer loyalty Creating summary columns and calculating descriptive statistics Visualizing the data in the form of various charts and graphs to more clearly understand relationships between variables

For a granular breakdown of all the steps, please see the GitHub repository for this project, as well as the final project report.

4. Findings and recommendations

Optimize advertising to target days (Tuesdays + Wednesdays) and times (23:00 - 06:00) when orders are lowest

Create a simpler price segmentation for further analysis, given that most items purchased are under $15

Offer incentives to returning customers (10+ orders) to turn them into loyal customers (over 40 orders)

Highlight promotions to increase basket size
